# Heat Transfer

**Heat transfer** is the movement of thermal energy from one system to another.

## Three Modes

### 1. Conduction

Heat transfer through a solid material via molecular collisions.

**Fourier's Law**:

$$q = -k \frac{dT}{dx}$$

Where:
- $q$ = heat flux (W/m²)
- $k$ = thermal conductivity
- $\frac{dT}{dx}$ = temperature gradient

### 2. Convection

Heat transfer between a surface and a moving fluid.

**Newton's Law of Cooling**:

$$q = hA(T_s - T_\infty)$$

Where:
- $h$ = convective heat transfer coefficient
- $A$ = surface area
- $T_s$ = surface temperature
- $T_\infty$ = fluid temperature

Types:
- **Natural convection** — fluid motion by buoyancy
- **Forced convection** — fluid motion by external means (fan, pump)

### 3. Radiation

Heat transfer via electromagnetic waves. Requires no medium.

**Stefan-Boltzmann Law**:

$$q = \varepsilon \sigma A (T^4 - T_{\text{surr}}^4)$$

Where:
- $\varepsilon$ = emissivity (0 to 1)
- $\sigma$ = Stefan-Boltzmann constant ($5.67 \times 10^{-8}$ W/m²·K⁴)
- $T$ = absolute temperature of the surface
- $T_{\text{surr}}$ = absolute temperature of surroundings

## Comparison

| Mode | Medium | Mechanism | Equation |
|------|--------|-----------|----------|
| Conduction | Solid | Molecular vibration | $q = -k \nabla T$ |
| Convection | Fluid | Bulk motion | $q = h\Delta T$ |
| Radiation | None | EM waves | $q = \varepsilon\sigma T^4$ |

## Heat Equation

The general equation for heat conduction in a solid:

$$\frac{\partial T}{\partial t} = \alpha \nabla^2 T + \frac{\dot{q}}{ \rho c_p}$$

Where $\alpha = \frac{k}{\rho c_p}$ is the **thermal diffusivity**.
