
# The First Law of Thermodynamics in Molecular Dynamics (MD)

In Molecular Dynamics (MD), the **First Law of Thermodynamics** dictates that the total energy of an isolated, conservative system is conserved. Energy is neither created nor destroyed, only transferred.

---

## The Core Equation

For a closed thermodynamic system, the change in internal energy ($\Delta U$) is the difference between heat added ($Q$) and work done ($W$):

$$ \Delta U = Q - W $$

---

## How the First Law Operates in MD

MD simulations utilize Newtonian mechanics to model atomic interactions. In an isolated system, energy continuously fluctuates between kinetic and potential forms, but the total energy remains constant:

$$E_{\text{total}} = K + V = \text{constant}$$

### Energy Components:
*   **Kinetic Energy ($K$):** Represents the motion of the atoms.
    $$ K = \frac{1}{2} \sum_{i=1}^{N} m_i v_i^2 $$
*   **Potential Energy ($V$):** Represents interatomic forces and interactions (e.g., chemical bonds, van der Waals forces, electrostatic interactions).

---

## System Ensembles & Temperature Control

To match real-world thermodynamic conditions, MD simulations use statistical mechanics "ensembles." These ensembles modify how the First Law is managed:

*   **NVE Ensemble (Microcanonical):** 
    *   Represents a completely isolated system.
    *   The number of particles ($N$), volume ($V$), and total energy ($E$) remain constant.
*   **NVT Ensemble (Canonical):** 
    *   The system connects to an external heat bath.
    *   Temperature ($T$) is held constant via a thermostat (e.g., Nosé-Hoover).
    *   Heat is exchanged with the surroundings, causing $E_{\text{total}}$ to fluctuate while the average temperature remains fixed.
*   **NPT Ensemble (Isothermal-Isobaric):** 
    *   Maintains constant temperature ($T$) and pressure ($P$).
    *   The simulation box volume fluctuates, explicitly accounting for the work ($W$) done by or on the system.
