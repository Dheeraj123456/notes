# Probability Theory

**Probability** quantifies the likelihood of events occurring.

## Basic Rules

For events $A$ and $B$:

$$P(A \cup B) = P(A) + P(B) - P(A \cap B)$$

$$P(A|B) = \frac{P(A \cap B)}{P(B)}$$

## Conditional Probability

The probability of $A$ given $B$:

$$P(A|B) = \frac{P(A \cap B)}{P(B)} = \frac{P(B|A) \cdot P(A)}{P(B)}$$

## Bayes' Theorem

$$P(A|B) = \frac{P(B|A) \cdot P(A)}{P(B)}$$

This is the foundation of Bayesian inference:

$$
\text{Posterior} = \frac{\text{Likelihood} \times \text{Prior}}{\text{Evidence}}
$$

## Probability Distributions

### Normal (Gaussian) Distribution

$$f(x) = \frac{1}{\sigma\sqrt{2\pi}} e^{-\frac{(x-\mu)^2}{2\sigma^2}}$$

- $\mu$: mean (center)
- $\sigma$: standard deviation (spread)
- 68-95-99.7 rule

### Binomial Distribution

$$P(X=k) = \binom{n}{k} p^k (1-p)^{n-k}$$

Number of successes in $n$ independent trials.

### Poisson Distribution

$$P(X=k) = \frac{\lambda^k e^{-\lambda}}{k!}$$

Number of events in a fixed interval.

## Law of Large Numbers

As the sample size increases, the sample mean converges to the expected value:

$$\bar{X}_n \xrightarrow{p} \mu$$

## Central Limit Theorem

The distribution of sample means approaches a normal distribution as $n$ increases, regardless of the population distribution.
